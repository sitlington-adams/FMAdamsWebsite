History of the Farm

 
 
 
 
 
1537-1946

Maugersbury Estate

Ash Farm is part of Maugersbury Estate

Our family are tenant farmers, starting with William Henry Powell (1825-1923)

His daughter, Winnie, marries Frank Adams and the farm business is called "Powell and Adams"

Frank and Winnie have two sons - Raymond and Gordon

19th September 1946

Ash Farm is purchased by Frank Adams

Auction held at the Unicorn Hotel, Stow-on-the-Wold at 3pm

Park Farm
Ash Farm (108 acres)
Smenham Farm
two Samll Holdings with Cottages
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
1949

Gordon Adams marries Margaret Fisher

They have one son, Martin

1962

Common Agricultural Policy

The CAP is introduced, its purpose is to increase productivity, to ensure a fair standard of living for communities employed in agriculture; to stabilise markets; to secure the availability of supplies; and to enforce fair prices.

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
1969

Raymond Adams marries Janette Sawtell

They have one son, Nicholas

1979

Martin Adams marries Kathryn Smith

They have three boys, Richard, Steven and Daniel

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
1984

Frank Adams dies

Raymond Adams and Gordon Adams take over the running of the farm

The business is called FM Adams and Sons

1990

Gordon and his son Martin buy-out Raymond

And purchase some additional land from the Banks farm estate

The business name is changed to FM Adams and Son

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
1990

BSE Crisis

The farm has no cases but new rules, such as the 30 month rule, are introduced for beef production

1995

Approved Waitrose and M&S Supplier

The farm is producing Aberdeen Angus cattle for Waitrose and Marks and Spencer

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
1996

Jay Barn is converted into a holiday cottage

The first barn conversion

2001

Foot and Mouth Crisis

Thousands of animals are slaughtered to prevent the spread of the disease

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
2000's

Further barn conversions

The Drey, The Warren and the other cottages are gradually converted

2010's

New agricultural buildings

Additional barns are erected, greatly expanding the farm's hay and straw storage capacity

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
7th May 2016

Gordon Adams dies aged 95

He worked well into his 90's

Martin and his wife Kate are running the farm

201?

Cotswold Forage

Steven Adams starts operating a forage delivery business from the farm

Specialising in the delivery of feed and bedding products to the racing industry

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
201?

Bed and Breakfast

Daniel Adams and wife Chantal start offering bed and breakfasts to complement the self catered accomodation already available
